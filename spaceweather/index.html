This week in space

Three reasons to look up

A storm is coming — the good kind

NOAA analysts say a G2-class geomagnetic storm is possible on July 3rd, when a CME from the June 30th X-flare reaches Earth. Aurora visibility could stretch to mid-latitudes in the dark hours around midnight.

Night-shining clouds over Europe

Sky watchers report natural noctilucent clouds — summertime plumes of water vapor that crystallize around meteor dust at the edge of space, glowing electric blue long after sunset.

A rocket drew on the sky

A Falcon 9 out of Vandenberg left an icy exhaust spiral visible 500 miles away, and delivered 24 more Starlink satellites — bringing the total in orbit to 10,746.
